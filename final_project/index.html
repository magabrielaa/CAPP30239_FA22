Disparity among the poor

As expected with individuals living under poverty, most are unemployed. However, the proportion of females unemployed is more significant than that of males.

Among the employed, the majority of men are day laborers, private sector workers or self-employed.

In the case of employed women under poverty, most are self-employed, followed by day labor, which speaks to the precariousness of jobs that lack stability and social protection.

Fewer females in poverty have access to social security

There is a marked difference in access to social security among the poor. 52,439 females have access to a social welfare compared to 85,541 males.

Not only are there more females living under poverty but also fewer of them are protected by the State.
