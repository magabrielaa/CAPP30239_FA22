Poverty in Ecuador: An Overview

The pandemic reverted a 10 year decline in poverty

Ecuador's poverty, as a percentage of the population, was on a declining trend since 2009.

The COVID-19 pandemic increased poverty back to 2009-2012 levels, peaking in 2020.

Rural areas have been the most affected, with almost half (49%) falling under poverty.

Signs of Recovery?

2021 shows a decline in poverty across National, Urban, and Rural levels. However, with economic instability, crime, and inequality on the rise in 2022, returning to 2010's development will take time.

Disparity among the poor

As expected with individuals living under poverty, most are unemployed. However, the proportion of females unemployed is more significant than that of males.

Among the employed, the majority of men are day laborers, private sector workers or self-employed.

In the case of employed women under poverty, most are self-employed, followed by day labor, which speaks to the precariousness of jobs that lack stability and social protection.

Fewer females in poverty have access to social security

There is a marked difference in access to social security among the poor. 52,439 females have access to a social welfare compared to 85,541 males.

Not only are there more females living under poverty but also fewer of them are protected by the State.

Indigenous peoples are gravely affected by extreme poverty

If we look at total poverty (including moderate and extreme poverty), the gap between mestizos and indigenous peoples is marked.

Moderate and extreme poverty

This gap shrinks when we focus on extreme poverty, with 56,192 indigenous peoples living under extreme poverty compared to 82,580 mestizos.

Extreme poverty

Why is this concerning?

72% of the population self-identifies as mestizo compared to only 7% self-identifies as indigenous.

This means that indigenous peoples are a minority drastically affected by extreme poverty.

Indigenous populations are concentrated in rural areas, which have also been particularly affected by the pandemic.
